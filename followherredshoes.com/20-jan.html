Inauguration Day!!

Inauguration Day!!January 20, 2017 jjvogelwalcutt
Uncategorized
0 Comment

Whether you voted for this administration or not, this is history and when you have the opportunity to be part of it, it is well worth the time and effort. We landed late in DC the night before so we were tired and therefore got up late. We left just two hours before the inauguration. We had no trouble taking the metro to the mall (the space before the capital building where us regular people get to stand for free) 😉

ME and my friend and colleague, Director of the Advanced Distributed Learning Initiative under the Office of the Secretary of Defense

There were lines and it wasn’t clear where to go but eventually we figured out that you had to go through basic security – in single file – to get into the mall. We arrived about 1 hour before the show began and there was plenty of space once inside the cordoned area. Most people were clearly strong Trump supporters based on the chants and enthusiasm they displayed.

People were excited – they often said how happy they were for the change – the chance for America to take back the country. Red, white, and blue was everywhere! As we approached the time to start, music started and a video looped on the screens for about 30 minutes – everyone got closer together and ready.

Once Trump took office and started his speech, the people around us were very excited – chatting and yelling wishes for change and to let out a lot of anger. It was an excited and aggressive crowd. Hillary and other democrats were heavily booed and at yelled at. We could hear riots outside the boundaries and because I know people on details, I was getting updates on safety issues and places to avoid.

          

After the inauguration, it started to rain so we skipped the parade and got coffee instead. From there…it was time to run home and change for the ball!!

SO much fun at the ball!! It was like going to prom but with a military bend to it. We felt like belles J We posed, we drank, we ate, we danced!! We met a ton of Trump supporters – everyone there was excited for the big changes to come.
