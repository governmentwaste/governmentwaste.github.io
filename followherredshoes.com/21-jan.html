Women’s March

Women’s MarchJanuary 21, 2017 jjvogelwalcutt
Uncategorized
0 Comment

SHENANIGANS!!  Because we are government employees, my colleague and I decided not to march but DID decide to observe. We left about 6 hours before the march was to begin to meet with friends for coffee. We couldn’t BELIEVE what we saw at the metro station. We BARELY squeezed on to the first train. We were so packed that by the first stop when more people tried to board, I was screaming from being crushed!!! Two women grabbed by hands and held me upright because I totally lost my ability to stand on my own. They pulled me close and began to pray. It was very moving.

As we departed, the scene was truly unbelievable – women EVERYWHERE!! And we were miles from the actual spot where the march was to begin and end. I was near tears the entire time we were there. We did not get coffee – we could hardly move there were so many people.

The scene was upbeat but obviously void of any security or organization. Though there were some signs against Trump, the absolute majority focused on supporting women. A young girl sitting on her mom’s shoulders was yelling, “TELL ME WHAT DEMOCRACY LOOKS LIKE!!!” and the crowd replied “THIS IS WHAT DEMOCRACY LOOKS LIKE!!!!” I have never seen so many people in my entire life in one place. The energy today was positive, supporting, upbeat, and determined. WOW.

 

 

We left early – about 2 hours before it was supposed to start but the pictures tell everything  – it was PACKED. We ran home to change into business attire….we were going to attend the Women in Politics meeting to discuss how to promote women. This group was focused on support and solutions whereas the march was about a statement. Given our positions in government, we felt that a solution-focused meeting would be a good way for us to learn more. And yes, we have to pay for this too!! HAHA  What did we see?

We listened to an UN (United Nations) Ambassador speak of her time overseas and the struggles for women. We learned about how hard it is for women to run for office. We heard authors read original poetry that blew me away!!        

Such power, such insight, such determination to help improve our country! This meeting was not at all what I expected in that it was so much more. I figured we would talk and problem solve but instead, I was inspired and encouraged. I was amazed by brilliance and excited to see women from both parties working together. It was another truly moving event!!!
