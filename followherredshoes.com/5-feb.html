Time to shop – Time to meet delegates

Time to shop – Time to meet delegatesFebruary 5, 2017 jjvogelwalcutt
Uncategorized
0 Comment

Though I am not a shopper in the states, I very much enjoy seeing different shopping spaces in other countries. So much is similar to the US, yet so much is different. In Finland, like many European countries, there are these wonderful little cafes that sell the most beautiful bread products – pretzel bread, croissants, sweet round breads covered in salty/sugar mixes and filled with sweet cream frosting, and of course, baguettes with meats and cheeses stuffed into the open breads.

One interesting common lunch food is to combine a large mound of mini shrimp with a side of hard-boiled egg and a lemon wedge – called a prawn and egg sandwich. A common treat seen everywhere in little shops is an almond rum cake in the shape of a cylinder with a dollop of fruit jam – called a Runebergintorttu (Runeberg’s torte).

 

   

At night, we had a meet-n-greet. There was a lovely spread of cheeses and meats. Wine was plenty. The coolest gadget was a little plate edge glass holder so that you can fill your food plate while also holding your wine but without requiring an extra hand….simple and brilliant! It is interesting to watch countries interact.

Clearly, many of the folks in this group already know each other. That said, countries generally group separately based on region. North America, Europe, Eastern Europe….and separate further by confidence in English. While everyone has very good English skills in speaking and writing, confidence in their abilities appears to affect their willingness to discuss and interact.

What did I learn? Every country wants to be second!
