What do we actually DO at a NATO meeting???

February 8, 2017 jjvogelwalcutt
Uncategorized
0 Comment

It was never so clear to me how similar and yet dissimilar our nations are. We all have families and beautiful pictures of kids with excellent and yet sometimes unruly behavior. We share similar issues with our militaries in that we struggle to attract the best talent and retain it. We struggle to match individuals to their best positions and ensure that they receive the most effective and efficient training.

Gathering requirements across countries through a note exercise

Yet, with different languages and different cultural traits when it comes to business, we struggle more to find a common ground for how to discuss things (process) and how to complete a clear task. For countries that value a system that doesn’t involve speaking up in big groups, it is easy to feel unheard. For individuals who are less confident in English, it is easier to observe than participate. And for countries like the US, it is easy to be misperceived as bulldozers because well, quite frankly, anyone at this level of our government is the type of person that fought many battles and won many arguments to get here – so of course we are the types to speak up! And we speak up frequently!

Given that the US is the country that spends the most money on NATO, well, we *could* control conversations. But the real question is – Is that wise? Just because you *can* monopolize doesn’t mean you *should.* I remember in Spiderman the movie when the uncle says…..”With great power comes great responsibility.” And so I think – it is important to empower others and really listen to what they are saying. Simply because a country is smaller does not at all mean they have less to share, nor does it mean they have less value.

Beautiful….but freezing…lake

“Morning coffee”….who puts guacamole on their crossiants????!!!

My lunch fav….salmon sashimi – I piled it HIGH – EVERYDAY!! 😀

  

If we want to defend like a world-class force and eradicate terrorism, then we must, WE MUST, learn to work together. Gestalt is a German word that means – the whole is greater than the sum of the parts. I believe it is quite fitting here. We need to work together to ensure safety across our globe – that is an achievement that is good for all our nations.

 

Lesson learned: Focus on “interoperability” – when technology, training programs, and rules follow the same standards and specifications. This allows for all nations, academies, and industry partners to contribute to development of training programs and optimizes the effectiveness and efficiency of that development.

 

It’s been a looooooonnnnggggg day….I’m wiped out!!!

The morning was focused on developing a clear vision of terminology across countries. WOWSA! This is a tough thing to do! So many issues!!

A) These are words that are not available in the dictionary because they are being developed through extending science. B) These are words that are being hotly debated in the research literature. C) NOW let’s discuss language and interpretation differences! OMG <sigh> It’s a LOT to debate, consider, discuss, argue, reconsider. In the end, we assigned a small group to do it on behalf of the masses. Oh my!

 

Now it’s time to swim in the freezing water, use the sauna, ice skate on the lake, ice sled, and make Finnish brats on an open fire. SO FUN!! I’m an avid ice skater and up until I took this job, was skating weekly….so I “know” what I’m doing. NOT! In Finland, they use the cross country skates on the lakes.

     

These skates have blades longer than your feet AND you have to skate on bumpy black ice. And when you notice the cracks in the lake – OMG – you look down, get your blade caught in one and see your life reviewed before you as you find that you are stuck and going down…hard….onto….the black ice.  And you find yourself praying a very quick prayer that you will not go THROUGH the ice. Oh my! It was fun and beautiful and freeing. The lake view was amazing!

 

Next….ice fishing. Get your maggot, string it alive, and place in a tiny hole that keeps refreezing as you’re sitting there. This begins to appear to be a very ill-thought idea quite quickly. I mean, it’s freezing outside, the fish are dormant, and everyone is staring at you raising and lowering the fishing hook….waiting….waiting…waiting….so I say, “OK, so how long does this take? And what does one do exactly while sitting here????” “We drink.” Ahhhhhhh……right. HAHA so I say to the lovely chap – “Well, hook me up!” Hot chocolate with crème de menthe…..Perfect!!

       

Now time for International Food and Beverage night…..this is essentially like going to Epcot.  A drink and snack from each country. It’s quite funny when two countries bring the same and claim it as their own! HA! It seems that Jagermeister is quite common in Europe 😉

And chocolate….oh chocolate. So much Swiss and Belgium chocolate. I believe though that I have exceeded my sausage intake for the rest of 2017 so Check Check on that one!

Time to work….Americans are Bulldozers???

Who knew I’d get a cool little flag next to my name! This is like being in the movies!! Mr. Chairman calls the meeting to order – so much formality! I am amazed. We discuss the schedule of the week and off we go! What I can’t figure out is – should I speak up? Should I remain quiet? This will be the theme of the entire week – the internal struggle to help and be involved but the realization that with the language barriers, it is necessary to wait longer than you typically would and to encourage everyone else to speak.

Many of the eastern countries tell me that it is not as common in their culture to speak up to fight for a position. Other countries, clearly are comfortable speaking more than others. It seems that a major differential remains between those who are confident in their English skills and those that are not. Also, clearly, we should know more French. It is a very commonly known language in Europe and Americans are at a clear disadvantage because we do not know it.

 

  

Dinner at a rustic traditional Finnish restaurants. What is a cloudberry anyway???? There was salmon soup and reindeer in mashed potatoes….and some sort of firm pudding with cloudberries – they look and taste a bit like pomegranate seeds. Interesting…

Then it was off to see the Helsinki city lights – we stopped at a famous military bar to hear the history and to sign the secret military book behind the bar.  Champagne and views from the sky over the city…..freezingly perfect…..

 

Lesson of the day: Avoid stories when making your point. It just creates a long discussion in English that one has to follow and typically makes it MORE difficult to understand the point rather than reaching the actual goal of clarifying.

Time to shop – Time to meet delegates

February 5, 2017 jjvogelwalcutt
Uncategorized
0 Comment

Though I am not a shopper in the states, I very much enjoy seeing different shopping spaces in other countries. So much is similar to the US, yet so much is different. In Finland, like many European countries, there are these wonderful little cafes that sell the most beautiful bread products – pretzel bread, croissants, sweet round breads covered in salty/sugar mixes and filled with sweet cream frosting, and of course, baguettes with meats and cheeses stuffed into the open breads.

One interesting common lunch food is to combine a large mound of mini shrimp with a side of hard-boiled egg and a lemon wedge – called a prawn and egg sandwich. A common treat seen everywhere in little shops is an almond rum cake in the shape of a cylinder with a dollop of fruit jam – called a Runebergintorttu (Runeberg’s torte).

 

   

At night, we had a meet-n-greet. There was a lovely spread of cheeses and meats. Wine was plenty. The coolest gadget was a little plate edge glass holder so that you can fill your food plate while also holding your wine but without requiring an extra hand….simple and brilliant! It is interesting to watch countries interact.

Clearly, many of the folks in this group already know each other. That said, countries generally group separately based on region. North America, Europe, Eastern Europe….and separate further by confidence in English. While everyone has very good English skills in speaking and writing, confidence in their abilities appears to affect their willingness to discuss and interact.

What did I learn? Every country wants to be second!

So how DO you get travel approval in government???

February 1, 2017 jjvogelwalcutt
Uncategorized
0 Comment

What a great question!! Did you know that people are often hired under one department but actually working under another? I work for the Office of Personnel Management but I am on loan to the Department of Defense. Why? In this case because I am performing a duty that is regulated by OPM but I’m performing it in support of the DoD. In other words, I have expertise in an area that OPM’s The Lab helps cultivate and oversee (human innovation) but I am using that expertise to help DoD’s ADL Initiative. Government: It gets confusing. 😉

So….travel…..your home department approves it BUT when your home department doesn’t frequently approval DoD travel, it becomes tricky. Money moves from one department to another and then the receiving department decides if you can go or not. The goal is to keep everything clean and clear. But the reality is that unless one is their own advocate, you can become lost in the shuffle. In the end, I got my approval just hours before my trip to Finland was to start because the acting Director at OPM was on the Hill and, not surprisingly, very busy. With so many positions vacate and no ability for them to be filled, everyone is running as fast as they can to do the best work they can for America.

Thank you to those that helped me and I hope Americans appreciate the work these great folks do. It’s an honor to serve and support our country – it’s imperative that we show the world who we really are. By attending NATO meetings, we have the opportunity to share good things about our people.

Heading home…what’s next?

Uncategorized

To leave DC is to remember our roots, our government, and in this case, try to foresee the future. I spent today at the Pentagon – it was a ghost town. Everyone has left and few new faces have yet to emerge. The acting head of our department quit promptly at 9am this morning in our very first meeting. Many others announced their retirements. Executive Orders were announced through twitter (the way even us in the government receive our information). It looks like the administration will be making a lot of changes. As I drive by the Lincoln Memorial, I can’t help but to reflect on our country and all that it has weathered. Our presidents have been of many different types but this new one will be the first to hold office without any knowledge or experience in government. What will he shake up? What changes will he make? What will a fresh set of eyes see? In some ways, we are two peas in a pod, no? We are both coming into this space ready to change the world – ready to influence government. How will our perspectives converge and diverge? He is at the top – I am surprisingly close enough to see so much but without the burden of everyone watching my every move. Well, it seems, President Trump, that you and I will traverse the same sea over the next four years – but from different levels, different backgrounds, and different skill sets. What will we see?

Women’s March

January 21, 2017 jjvogelwalcutt
Uncategorized
0 Comment

SHENANIGANS!!  Because we are government employees, my colleague and I decided not to march but DID decide to observe. We left about 6 hours before the march was to begin to meet with friends for coffee. We couldn’t BELIEVE what we saw at the metro station. We BARELY squeezed on to the first train. We were so packed that by the first stop when more people tried to board, I was screaming from being crushed!!! Two women grabbed by hands and held me upright because I totally lost my ability to stand on my own. They pulled me close and began to pray. It was very moving.

As we departed, the scene was truly unbelievable – women EVERYWHERE!! And we were miles from the actual spot where the march was to begin and end. I was near tears the entire time we were there. We did not get coffee – we could hardly move there were so many people.

The scene was upbeat but obviously void of any security or organization. Though there were some signs against Trump, the absolute majority focused on supporting women. A young girl sitting on her mom’s shoulders was yelling, “TELL ME WHAT DEMOCRACY LOOKS LIKE!!!” and the crowd replied “THIS IS WHAT DEMOCRACY LOOKS LIKE!!!!” I have never seen so many people in my entire life in one place. The energy today was positive, supporting, upbeat, and determined. WOW.

 

 

We left early – about 2 hours before it was supposed to start but the pictures tell everything  – it was PACKED. We ran home to change into business attire….we were going to attend the Women in Politics meeting to discuss how to promote women. This group was focused on support and solutions whereas the march was about a statement. Given our positions in government, we felt that a solution-focused meeting would be a good way for us to learn more. And yes, we have to pay for this too!! HAHA  What did we see?

We listened to an UN (United Nations) Ambassador speak of her time overseas and the struggles for women. We learned about how hard it is for women to run for office. We heard authors read original poetry that blew me away!!        

Such power, such insight, such determination to help improve our country! This meeting was not at all what I expected in that it was so much more. I figured we would talk and problem solve but instead, I was inspired and encouraged. I was amazed by brilliance and excited to see women from both parties working together. It was another truly moving event!!!
