Inauguration Day!!

January 20, 2017 jjvogelwalcutt
Uncategorized
0 Comment

Whether you voted for this administration or not, this is history and when you have the opportunity to be part of it, it is well worth the time and effort. We landed late in DC the night before so we were tired and therefore got up late. We left just two hours before the inauguration. We had no trouble taking the metro to the mall (the space before the capital building where us regular people get to stand for free) 😉

ME and my friend and colleague, Director of the Advanced Distributed Learning Initiative under the Office of the Secretary of Defense

There were lines and it wasn’t clear where to go but eventually we figured out that you had to go through basic security – in single file – to get into the mall. We arrived about 1 hour before the show began and there was plenty of space once inside the cordoned area. Most people were clearly strong Trump supporters based on the chants and enthusiasm they displayed.

People were excited – they often said how happy they were for the change – the chance for America to take back the country. Red, white, and blue was everywhere! As we approached the time to start, music started and a video looped on the screens for about 30 minutes – everyone got closer together and ready.

Once Trump took office and started his speech, the people around us were very excited – chatting and yelling wishes for change and to let out a lot of anger. It was an excited and aggressive crowd. Hillary and other democrats were heavily booed and at yelled at. We could hear riots outside the boundaries and because I know people on details, I was getting updates on safety issues and places to avoid.

          

After the inauguration, it started to rain so we skipped the parade and got coffee instead. From there…it was time to run home and change for the ball!!

SO much fun at the ball!! It was like going to prom but with a military bend to it. We felt like belles J We posed, we drank, we ate, we danced!! We met a ton of Trump supporters – everyone there was excited for the big changes to come.

It’s that time again!! I/ITSEC (www.iitsec.org; largest modeling and simulation conference in the world). So how does a conference get planned? A TON of work and a lot of planning. If you participate in the planning as a government or military worker….you PAY FOR YOUR OWN TRAVEL to support!! If you are in industry, your company pays. Starting about a year ahead, these conferences require individuals from all areas around the world to participate in the choosing topics, locations, logistics – and then picking papers and reviewing them for quality, regulating everything. Cost runs about $00-$700/pp to attend, depending on the conference. In this one, military and government can attend for free – just the planning support costs you for the travel.

Orlando is the epicenter of modeling and simulation because we have all military branches present, the University of Central Florida which is a national leader in modeling and sim, and we have Disney and other industries pushing simulation for entertainment. Otronicon is an event for students to come and see how simulation can be fun and exciting and change the way you learn. From preschool through college, kids can do everything from ride in a simulated roller coaster to control robots to complete engineering tasks to conduct large scale, network-based military simulation exercises. The youth of today are being prepped for the engineering revolution of tomorrow!!!  

Military Breakfast meetings: Military Interoperability

January 13, 2017 jjvogelwalcutt
Uncategorized
0 Comment

ME, Navy, Army, and Military Outreach

Ahhhh…..we eat….ON OUR OWN PERSONAL DIME hahaha and meet. If you work for a company, they pay for your breakfast but if you work for the military or government, you pay your own way. Interesting…  In attendance are several defense industry partners in Orlando but the showcase is of the four branches of military – particularly focused on training and education. The primary goal of this meeting is to discuss some of the challenges facing our defense training community. Of highest priority is interoperability – or the ability for computer and network based training systems to be able to share data. Why? Because a) if they can’t, then every branch and every topic area has to have it’s own training system. This is very expensive. And b) when systems can share data about a person, then each system can be individualized to meet the trainee’s needs. In other words, it will save massive money and time if systems of the future can be interoperable.
