Every wonder how government pays for office space???? Well, oftentimes, WE DON’T! There is so much talk of waste in the government but there are oftentimes so many places where we are highly efficient and no one even knows. So in this case, our governement lab (ADL) is partners with academia (colleges), industry (companies), and local military (all four branches) to form what’s called Team Orlando. We are a high tech hub for modeling and simulation and for cyber. The University of Central Florida has one of the first PhD programs in behavioral cyber and they are the reigning champions for collegiate cyber competitions. In 2016, our high school kids also took top honors at the national level in Army and Navy. My daughter was on the campion team!! Hoorah Centurion Battalion!! So ANYWAY….right…paying for space. Under normal circumstances, the company to whom we contract our workers pays for the office space. Sometimes, we pay. But when you are part of a team like this, the university pays for the building and government and military associated labs get to use the space for FREE!!!

  Global partnerships are imperative to the safety and efficiency of our government. We share ideas, we share resources….and if we are going to defend our nations together, we must learn to train together. Let the excitement commence!!

November 28, 2016 jjvogelwalcutt
Uncategorized
0 Comment

It’s time for the floor of simulators to be opened. And to start these festivities, Congressman Bobby Scott tours the facilities and we get the opportunity to explain the benefits of open source. When companies make their platforms open source, everyone has the opportunity to build new applications using it. This is highly beneficial because it means we can connect work across the world and the market can pay based on applications that are built rather than underlying platforms. Further, it means individuals or companies that aren’t equipped to build platforms but are able to use their content expertise to build applications can do so. Efficiency is expected!

I/ITSEC Conference

November 26, 2016 jjvogelwalcutt
Uncategorized
0 Comment

IITSEC (Interservice/Industry Training, Simulation and Education Conference) “is the world’s largest modeling, simulation, and training conference” (www.iitsec.org) and is hosted every fall in Orlando, Florida. No surprise – I present here every year. But this year is different….I am a govvie. I will be introduced. I will sit at the big girls table. I will be someone people want to talk to. Wow!

If you’ve never been to a large conference like this before, let me tell you, just arriving is overwhelming. There are always meetings to be in, talks to listen to, and exhibits with which to play. There are also lots of candies and at night, drinks and food. The first day is rather uneventful. But the packing was a major ordeal! I needed ball gowns and soooo many fancy clothes. Even though it was in Orlando, it met the 22 mile requirement to be considered government travel so I got to stay on site. This was IMPORTANT. We will run from 7am to midnight every day. Driving on top of that schedule is quite frankly, dangerous.

Out of the starting gate, I realize the world has changed. I’m talking to nearly no one under the rank of Colonel or Captain. And every time I talk to someone, they are the director of something or other. Why does this matter? As human beings, it doesn’t really. But if you want to change the world – then you have to sit with the decision makers. So today, it really sunk in, I’m in a space where I can affect the world if I want to. Do I want to? YES!!  Let’s do this!
