Departments of Energy and Transportation: Who wants innovation?!

Departments of Energy and Transportation: Who wants innovation?!February 27, 2017 jjvogelwalcutt
Uncategorized
0 Comment

Written obscurely on a small well site (http://www.trb.org/AboutTRB/AboutTRB.aspx), the Department of   Transportation makes minor, but important, reference to the research board that designs innovative ideas for transportation. WHY ISN’T THIS EMPHASIZED FOR AMERICA??? OF COURSE we should be bringing in the latest ideas for how to develop our infrastructure. If we have ANY hopes of keeping up with the rest of the world, the notion that “it worked for me so it will work for you” must be put to rest. Innovation is the hallmark of progress and many of the smaller, more agile countries have the ability to out run us in this area. Their size acts to their advantage.

Meanwhile….the Department of Energy’s National Training Center (https://ntc.doe.gov/) calls me to discuss innovative ways to improve their training. Is it enough, you should ask yourself, to teach basic safety? Or would you feel safer if our individuals at nuclear power plants were practicing in simulators to address possible cyber attacks and technology failures? With only basic elearning practices (taking online courses), there is a limit to what can be taught and practiced. But in my lab and in my position, we can share the latest in learning innovation and improve all these departments!
