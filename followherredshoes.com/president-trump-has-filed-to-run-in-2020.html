President Trump – has filed to run in 2020????

President Trump – has filed to run in 2020????February 14, 2017 jjvogelwalcutt
Uncategorized
0 Comment

First, Happy Valentines to all!! – From me and my littlest Valentine 😉

Second….two memorandums today surfaces on my desk: Guidance of President Trump’s Status as a Candidate and Its Effect on Activity in the Federal Workplace (from the U.S. Office of Special Counsel) and Naming Convention for the Islamic State of Iraq and Syria (from the Office of the Secretary of Defense). Wow!

And I quote, “President Trump has filed paperwork with the Federal Election Commission establishing that he is a candidate in the 2020 Presidential election.” Well that’s interesting….  It continues to say, “…the Hatch Act does not prohibit federal employees, either on duty or off duty, from….expressing their approval or disapproval of President Trump or his Administration, policies, or actions.”  Note: The Hatch Act (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hatch_Act_of_1939) prohibits federal employees from running for partisan office and from openly supporting or not supporting candidates. We can vote, we just can’t express our opinions at work. But it turns out, that is only for candidates – not already elected officials who are working. So just so I’m clear…I can cheer or complain as often as I like at work or anywhere else while the president (or his administration) is working….butttttt…..not while they are trying to win? Somehow this seems rather backward. It’s like saying…Don’t tell me what you think until AFTER the decisions have been made. Brilliant. Further, we expect people to have government experience but then disallow government workers to run for office. Again – what company would give preferential treatment to individuals with no working experience in their company and their company’s products??? There has to be a better answer.  What about a preliminary set of individuals who are pre-elected for the following term? In other words, what if in 2020 we elected a Pres/VP but also a set of 8-10 individuals from both parties who spend the next 2.5 years learning about government and planning their campaigns? Then we could encourage people without personal enormous fortunes and people who have expertise in something other than politics to run. I am officially dubbing it the Presidential Apprentice Program. (rather interesting choice of words given the current President’s former TV experience…) 😉

Now for ISIS… There was talk that the term was going to become DAESH but it seems that has not been adopted by President Trump. “Consistent with National Security Presidential Memorandum-3 of January 28, 2017, “Plan to Defeat the Islamic State of Iraq and Syria,”……the Department of Defense will use the term Islamic State of Iraq and Syria, or ISIS, when referring to this threat.”  Well, OK!

I’d like to say that these memorandums seem overly directive – but in truth, we have received little guidance and believe it or not, I get most of my information from Twitter. So I am pleased that we are receiving legal clarification regarding what we can and cannot say – in and out of the office.
